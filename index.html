CHAPTER I

Who Owns the Ground Truth?

The verification layer and the quiet capture of insurance and land rights

Every institution that manages physical risk rests on a quiet assumption: knowledge of the ground’s state over time is scarce and contestable. A decade of daily whole-Earth imaging, absorbed into foundation models, is deleting that scarcity. The consequence is not efficiency but capture — insurance and land rights do not collapse, but their verification layer relocates to whoever owns the record. Four dated trajectories to 2045; India as the proof-case, where the fork is decided first. The remedy: constitute the planetary record as sovereign digital public infrastructure before ownership hardens.

CHAPTER II

Old Before Rich

India and the first AI-mediated aging transition

Every economics of aging rests on a quiet assumption: societies grow rich before they grow old, so accumulated wealth pays for the machines and the care. India will break it. Its demographic dividend closes around the 2050s — decades before high income — making it the first large civilization to attempt an aging transition before wealth, and to attempt it on AI: automation as a simultaneous hedge against a shrinking young workforce and a caregiver deficit measured in millions. The chapter contests the practitioner consensus of AI-as-supplement, and proposes care as public infrastructure.
