# Gate-1 Abstract — Sanjay Gupta

# Topic: Old Before Rich
*India and the First AI-Mediated Aging Transition*

## Abstract

Every economics of aging rests on a quiet assumption: that societies grow rich before
they grow old, so accumulated wealth pays for the machines and the care. India will break
that assumption. Its demographic dividend closes around the 2050s — decades before it
reaches high income — making it the first large civilization to attempt an aging
transition **before wealth**, and to attempt it on AI: automation deployed as a
**simultaneous hedge** against two shortages at once, a shrinking young workforce and a
caregiver deficit measured in millions. The evidence that automation answers aging comes
only from rich agers — Japan, Germany, Korea (Acemoglu & Restrepo); China, where the
AI-against-the-demographic-trap argument is already mainstream, ages roughly three times
richer per head. India crosses the boundary in reverse. Whether that broadens opportunity
or hardens into a two-tier society — an AI-served elite above an excluded rural majority,
and women whose unpaid care AI displaces without alternative — is decided by design, not
by the technology's arrival. The chapter architects four dated trajectories (inertia,
two-tier capture, designed inclusion, substitution overshoot), argues India's scale and
cost ceiling force *substitution* — contesting the practitioner consensus (TCS) of
AI-as-supplement — and proposes **care as public infrastructure** as the designed-inclusion
path. Its novelty lies in the unstudied convergence of pre-rich aging, dual-hedge
automation, and distributional design. Falsifiable 2035/2050 anchors, with disconfirming
conditions, are stated in-chapter.
