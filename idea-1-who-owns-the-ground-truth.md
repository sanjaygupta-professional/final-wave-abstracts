# Gate-1 Abstract — Sanjay Gupta

# Topic: Who Owns the Ground Truth?
*The Verification Layer and the Quiet Capture of Insurance and Land Rights*

## Abstract

Every institution that manages physical risk rests on a quiet assumption: that knowledge
of the ground's state over time is scarce and contestable. A decade of daily whole-Earth
imaging, absorbed into foundation models, is deleting that scarcity — turning the planet's
past into a queryable, retroactive record. This chapter argues the consequence is not
efficiency but capture. Insurance and land rights do not collapse — capital still absorbs
catastrophe, the state still enforces title — but their **verification layer**, the trust
function that says what is true about the ground, relocates to whoever owns the record:
the observation monopoly becomes de facto insurer-of-record and registry-of-record at
once. The chapter architects four dated trajectories to 2045 — institutional inertia,
data feudalism, planetary commons, observation wars — and takes India as the proof-case:
the one state with the digital-public-infrastructure muscle (Aadhaar, SVAMITVA, PMFBY) to
choose commons over feudalism, and the place the fork is decided first. As a remedy, it
proposes the planetary record be constituted as **sovereign digital public
infrastructure** before ownership hardens. Its novelty lies in the unstudied convergence
of retroactive planetary observation, institutional trust functions, and
ownership-of-record. Falsifiable 2035/2045 anchors, with disconfirming conditions, are
stated in-chapter.
